History of Ice Hockey

The origin of ice hockey is unknown; however, ice hockey probably evolved from the game of field hockey has been played in Northern Europe for centuries. Discovering early sticks and ball games, is evidence that such a game has been played in ancient Egypt and Greece.

According to the International Ice Hockey Federation (IIHF), the first organized ice hockey game was played on March 1875. Since then, it has been always changing.

Canada has been a dominant figure in international competition.

Canada’s men national ice hockey team won the following medals in the Olympics games:

Gold medals: 1920, 1924. 1928, 1932, 1948, 1952, 2002, 2010, 2014, 2021

Silver medals: 1936, 1960, 1992, 1994

Bronze medals: 1956, 1968, 2018

The major winners of major ice hockey tournaments are Canada and the United States.

Women's Hockey

While the First World War was taking place (1915-1920), women in North America started playing field hockey.

The first organized women ice hockey in Canada date back to 1890s when it was played at the university level. Today, girls’ hockey is very widespread in North America.

The Canadian women ice hockey team representing Canada in the International Ice Hockey Federation (IIHF) is the Canadian women’s national ice hockey team. Women’s ice-hockey has two different levels of play at the IIHF:

  • The under-18 event
  • The senior women’s and Olympics
The greatest female hockey player of all times is Hayley Wickenheiser (born August 12, 1978, Saskatchewan, Canada), known for the most gold medals of any Canadian Olympian.
